As the commercial use of Unmanned Aerial Systems (UASs) for tasks such as surveillance, infrastructure maintenance, and goods delivery increases, the density of urban air traffic will exceed the capacity of traditional human control centers. To ensure safety and efficiency at scale, an automated unmanned approach to UAS Traffic Management (UTM) is required, in which Airspace Service Providers (ASPs) negotiate flight plans with operators without continuous human oversight.\par

\vspace{10pt}

This project addresses that challenge by treating urban airspace management as a Multi-Agent Path Finding (MAPF) problem and evaluating a range of management policies through simulation. A custom discrete event simulation framework was developed in Python using SimPy, capable of modelling one or more ASPs serving dynamic flight plan requests across a configurable urban grid.  \par

\vspace{10pt}

Eight policies were implemented and compared, ranging from uninformed search baselines (BFS, DFS) and a direct path policy that ignores obstacles and other agents to provide a lower bound reference on achievable flight time, through single agent informed search (A*) and a variant aware of dynamic occupancy (Occupancy A*), to prioritised and cooperative coordination strategies (Prioritised A*, Cooperative A*, and Windowed Hierarchical Cooperative A*). Each policy was evaluated under multiple demand levels and reservation configurations, with 5 independent random seeds per condition to support reproducible statistical comparison. \par

\vspace{10pt}

The evaluation produced three principal findings. First, the segment based reservation mechanism eliminates UAV collisions across the policies tested. However, it comes at a measurable cost in completion rate and average delivery time. Second, prioritised and cooperative methods outperform uninformed search baselines on safety and efficiency, but the dominant comparison between them depends on the demand level and reservation horizon rather than on the choice of search algorithm in isolation. Third, the completability of prioritised time expanded search is bounded its horizon rather than by available compute. \par

\vspace{10pt}

The results indicate that the principal driver of safe and efficient ASP-mediated UAS coordination is the presence of an explicit time aware coordination mechanism rather than the sophistication of the underlying single agent planner. Reservation based coordination is shown to be a practical and tractable alternative to full space-time MAPF for ASP mediated UTM. Directions for future work include the integration of coupled search methods such as Conflict Based Search, the evaluation of tactical reactive avoidance methods such as VOs, and the extension of the framework to three dimensional airspace.
